Task 1 · Market Attractiveness

Attractive, but mature, not growth.

◆ Why enter

  • World's 3rd-largest economy; high margins, GDP/capita ≈ $43k (p.9)
  • EU–Japan FTA scraps duties on 97% of EU goods (p.9)
  • Strong affinity for European quality & design (p.45)

▲ Why it's hard

  • Market shrinking ~379k/yr; 28.4% aged 65+ (p.10)
  • Saturated: a strong domestic incumbent in every category (p.10)
  • Unforgiving consumers, weak brand loyalty (p.9)

Japan's population has peaked and is declining

Aging, not just shrinking: working-age share is 59.5%, the lowest since 1950, while children are just 12.1%. (p.10–11)

Recreated from the report, Fig. 1 “Population Change in Japan” (p.11) & demographic data (p.10).

Verdict

Japan is a share-capture, niche-growth market. You win by taking share from incumbents in the pockets where wealth concentrates, not by riding a rising tide.

Task 2 · Understanding the Consumer

The world's most demanding buyers. Adapt or fail.

▲ What makes them different

  • World-class quality & service: one defect can lose a customer for good (p.14–15)
  • Brand-conscious, not loyal: they switch readily (p.15)
  • Novelty-hungry: new releases & limited editions (gentei) (p.16)
  • Obsessive researchers: social media #1; check Mercari resale first (p.17)

◆ What COVID accelerated (and will stay)

  • E-commerce & delivery: online spend +20–30% (p.24)
  • Cashless: ~40% use it more since 2020 (p.20)
  • Instagram discovery: #1 way to find products (54%) (p.25)
  • Home, health & ethical consumption (p.24–25)

Payment is fragmented and splits by age

Young lean cash + emerging cashless; older lean credit card. An entrant must accept them all: cash, card & mobile. (p.19–20)

Recreated from the report, Fig. 5 “Preferred Payment Methods” (p.20); “Other cashless” = debit + contactless + QR + IC card.

Imperative

No second chances: adapt product, service, packaging & payment to the Japanese bar, and reach them digitally, on Instagram.

Task 3 · Which Segment to Target

Target the silver market: that's where the wealth is.

◆ Primary target: Silver (65+)

  • Biggest wallet: 55+ hold 72% of financial assets (→79% by 2040) (p.27)
  • 28.8% of the population and still growing (p.26)
  • Pay more for quality & ethics; value after-sales (p.28)
  • Best fit for premium European goods (p.28)

◆ Growth hedge: Single-person households

  • ~35% of households → the largest group within 2 decades (p.42)
  • Spend freely vs married; cocooning & "me-time" (p.43)
  • Overlaps elderly singles: a silver strategy already reaches it (p.42)

Millennials (16%) & Gen Z (14%): large but frugal / low-income → not the premium priority (p.31, 37).

Wealth is concentrated in the old, and concentrating further

65+ are 28.8% of the people, but 55+ hold ~72% of the money. Japan's elderly own about half of the nation's $17 trillion in financial assets. (p.27)

Recreated from the report, Fig. 10 “Wealth Distribution in Japan by Age Group” (p.27).

Recommendation

Silver-led, singles-hedged: invest where the wealth sits and the growth is heading, not in the shrinking mainstream.

Task 4 · Recommendations

The strategy: three evidence-based moves.

01

Sell premium & authentically European

  • Target silver + singles: the wealth & the growth (p.27, 42)
  • European quality/design has a “very good sales prognosis” & high margins (p.45)
  • Charge for the authenticity: foreignness is a status asset (p.16, 45)
Report rec: Choose the Right Products
Effort · moderate
02

Adapt the whole mix: zero compromise

  • Flawless, consistent quality + extra QC; world-class after-sales (p.45)
  • Small packaging; accept cash + card + cashless (p.18, 20)
  • Lead discovery on Instagram & influencers (p.25)
Report recs: Customer is God + Adapt Product
Hardest · top priority
03

Enter patiently and keep it “new”

  • Budget ~3× the entry time; market-test first (p.45)
  • Use the EU-Japan Centre / chambers for support (p.45)
  • Run a pipeline of limited/seasonal editions (gentei) (p.16)
Report recs: Prepare Carefully + Be Ready for Change
Effort · moderate
Standardize or adapt?

Adapt everything, except your European authenticity. That's the one thing worth keeping foreign.

Task 5 · Critically Evaluating the Evidence ★

A credible map, but not a decision.

“
[This report] does not claim to be comprehensive or up to date… No person should rely on the contents… without first obtaining advice from a qualified professional.

The report's own disclaimer (p.56). By its own terms, it is orientation, not a decision-grade study.

◆ The evidence used

100% SECONDARY DATA

Primary research conducted: none. It's a desk synthesis.

  • Gov stats + consultancies (McKinsey, Euromonitor, Statista) (p.47–54)
  • News + academic, incl. the author's own 2010–11 work (p.49)
  • Segmented by age & gender only

▲ What's missing

  • No primary research: no surveys / interviews
  • No competitor or pricing data, yet competition is the #1 risk (p.10)
  • No product-category sizing
  • Partly pre-COVID · ~5 yrs stale (p.56)

◆ What we'd collect today

  • Primary voice-of-customer: surveys, interviews
  • Competitor + price benchmarking
  • Psychographics + conjoint / WTP
  • Social listening + predictive analytics; a real test-market
Bottom line

Use it as the starting map, then commission primary, competitor & analytics research before committing capital.
